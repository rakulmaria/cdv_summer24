The weather of the Faroe Islands

1973 - 2001

The weather of the Faroe Islands, is a creative data visualisation that seeks to show its viewers that a weather forecast can be translated into so much more than just numbers and statistics.

The data visualisation uses weather reports of the Faroe Islands from the year of 1973 - 2001 to create a colorful and creative poster-like visualisation. Each visualisation of a year contains the annual percipation, average annual temperature, number of days with storm, hail, snow and fog. Each of the different datapoints are translated into different shapes with different colours.

The visualisation seeks to inspire the viewer and show that a weather report can be so much more than just numbers and statistics; it can be translated into art.

The visualisation was developed as a part of the final exam at the summer course, Creative Data Visualisation, taught by Annelie Berner at the IT University of Copenhagen.

How to read the data?

The centuries are placed on the y-axis, ranging from 70s - 00s. Each year in a century is placed on the x-axis, rangin from x0 - x9. For example, the year of 1979 is placed at the top-right corner.

Total annual rain or snow percipation. The bigger the circle, the higher the percipation. Ranges from 1055 - 2084 PP.

Average annual temperature. Lowest temperature is blue, highest temperature is red. Ranges from 5.4 - 7.4°C.

Number of days with storm. Each yellow circle represents a day with storm. Ranges from 0 - 5 days.

Number of days with hail. Each light-blue line represents a day with hail. Ranges from 1 - 33 days.

Number of days with snow. Each white dot represents a day with snow. Ranges from 7 - 93 days.

Number of days with fog. The bigger the cloud, the more foggy days. Rotation of cloud is random. Ranges from 20 - 59 days.
